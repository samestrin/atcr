## Current State

PageRank authority promotion (epic 13.3) raises an isolated single-reviewer
finding from MEDIUM to HIGH when its model's run authority exceeds the 1/N
baseline (`reconcile/pagerank.go:181`, `promoteByAuthority`). The promotion is
**silent**: nothing in the run `Summary` records that a HIGH came from authority
rather than from reviewer count. A misfiring promotion is only derivable
indirectly as a "HIGH with a single reviewer."

## Why Deferred to v2

Adding a counter to `reconcile.Summary` (`reconcile/reconcile.go:38-65`) is a new
JSON/serialization field. The epic 13.3 scope boundary explicitly prohibits any
new wire/serialization field in v1 (the clarification locked the v1 Summary wire
schema), and a new field would change wire output and may break golden/snapshot
fixtures. This is intentionally held for a v2 epic where the wire schema is
allowed to evolve. The residual v1 risk is acceptable: promotion is promote-only
and never suppresses a finding.

## Proposed Solution

Add an `AuthorityPromoted int` field to `reconcile.Summary` (with a JSON tag,
e.g. `authority_promoted`) and increment it in `Reconcile`
(`reconcile/reconcile.go:106-115`) each time `promoteByAuthority` actually raises
a finding's confidence (i.e. the returned `Merged` flipped MEDIUMâ†’HIGH). Update
the golden/snapshot fixtures that serialize `Summary` to include the new field.
