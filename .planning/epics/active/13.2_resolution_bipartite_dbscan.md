# Feature Request: Bipartite Matching & DBSCAN

- **Estimated time**: 1 week
- **Tasks**: 3
- **Components**: 1
- **Execution**: execute-epic

## Context

Deduplication currently groups findings using a greedy clustering algorithm based on an arbitrary similarity threshold.

## Problem Statement

-   **Sub-optimal grouping**: Greedy clustering algorithms often group the wrong things or leave stragglers when matching multiple reviewers' outputs.
-   **Hallucination isolation**: Currently, identifying isolated "noise" from a single model relies on arbitrary proximity thresholds.

## Proposed Solution

1.  **Resolution Phase**: Implement the Kuhn-Munkres (Hungarian) algorithm to match Model A's findings to Model B's findings optimally. The edge weights for the bipartite graph are the similarity distances (derived from 13.0 NCD and 13.1 AST Isomorphism).
2.  **Noise Isolation**: Implement DBSCAN (Density-Based Clustering) over the resulting similarity matrix. Findings that fall outside the dense clusters are automatically and deterministically isolated into the `ambiguous.json` sidecar.

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] AC1: Reconciler deduplication pipeline rewritten to use Bipartite Matching.
- [ ] AC2: Benchmark tests prove Bipartite Matching resolves known greedy-clustering failure edge cases.
- [ ] AC3: DBSCAN successfully isolates known model hallucinations into `ambiguous.json` without requiring arbitrary `k` thresholds.

## Success Criteria

### Functional
- [ ] Deduplication optimally aligns findings between $N$ models.
- [ ] Hallucinations are cleanly separated from consensus findings.

### Business
- [ ] Increases end-user trust in the `ambiguous.json` sidecar by proving its contents are strictly uncorroborated noise.

## Technical Constraints

- Bipartite matching is $O(V^3)$. Must ensure $V$ (findings per file per model) remains small.

## Non-Functional Requirements
- Memory footprint of distance matrices must be minimal.

## Out of Scope
- Prompt-based LLM arbitration of the bipartite matching results.

## Dependencies
- 13.0 (NCD) and 13.1 (AST Isomorphism) metrics must be available to use as edge weights.

## Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|------------|--------|------------|
| Bipartite matching is overkill | Medium | Low | Profile execution time; $O(V^3)$ is generally negligible for $V<50$. |
