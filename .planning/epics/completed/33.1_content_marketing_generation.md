# Epic Plan 33.1: Launch Content Strategy & Auto-Generation

- **Estimated time**: TBD
- **Tasks/Components**: 3 / 1
- **Execution**: init-plan

## Objective

Leverage the completed engineering documentation to automatically generate a backlog of high-quality, top-of-funnel content marketing outlines. These outlines will fuel the product's launch and post-launch developer marketing strategy.

## Context

Developers and engineering managers respond best to marketing that speaks directly to a technical pain point and explains how a tool solves it. Rather than writing blog posts from scratch, ATCR already has the perfect seed data:
1. **The Epic Plans (`.planning/epics/completed/`):** Provide the narrative "Why" (the Problem Statement, Context, and Objective).
2. **The `CHANGELOG.md`:** Provides the concrete "What" and "How" (the high-level implementation details of what actually shipped).

By combining these two sources, an agent can synthesize compelling technical blog outlines that highlight features like the Multi-Agent Reconciler, Local Privacy Personas, and Benchmark Leaderboards.

## Proposed Solution

1. **Information Extraction:** Instruct an agent to iterate over the major completed epics in `.planning/epics/completed/`.
2. **Implementation Grounding:** For each epic, cross-reference the `CHANGELOG.md` to ground the narrative in the actual shipped features (avoiding vaporware claims).
3. **Outline Generation:** Author a structured Markdown outline for each major feature in `.planning/product/content/blog/`. Each outline should include:
   - The Hook (The industry pain point).
   - The Technical Challenge (Why it was hard to build or why existing solutions fail).
   - The ATCR Solution (How the feature solves it, using details from the CHANGELOG).
   - A Call to Action.

## Acceptance Criteria

- [x] The agent successfully reads the completed epics and the `CHANGELOG.md`.
- [x] At least 5-10 technical blog post outlines are generated in `.planning/product/content/blog/`.
- [ ] Each outline accurately reflects the shipped implementation (as per the CHANGELOG) rather than just the initial epic proposal.
