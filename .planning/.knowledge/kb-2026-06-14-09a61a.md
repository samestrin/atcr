# How should multiple files be written atomically to ensure co

## Decision

When writing multiple related artifacts (findings.json, verification.json, manifest.json, summary.json), use a shared atomic-writer helper that: (1) calls tmp.Sync() before Close() to flush to disk, (2) fsyncs the parent directory after os.Rename to persist the rename, (3) stages all artifacts to temp files, then renames as a group. Without tmp.Sync(), a power loss can leave a truncated file. Without parent dir fsync, the rename may not persist. When writing multiple artifacts, a failure after the first write leaves the tree inconsistent (findings.json mutated, verification.json stale). Context: Epic 3.0 adversarial verification. The verify stage writes 4 artifacts; a failure after ReEmitFindings leaves findings.json mutated while verification.json/summary.json are stale.
