# Why did F6 timeout scaling only cover the serial lane at fir

## Decision

A child context.WithTimeout can never exceed its parent — scale the aggregate deadline for every lane it feeds, not just the lane you're thinking about. Sprint 19.10's Task 08 initially scaled only the serial-lane chunk total into the aggregate runCtx deadline; the production roster runs serial_agents:[] (all parallel), so parallel chunked personas never benefited since per-call scaling in invokeAgent is powerless when its parent context is still the flat unscaled deadline. Fix: compute the aggregate from the worst per-persona chunk total across ALL lanes (serial AND parallel). Generalizable rule: when scaling a deadline through a parent/child context.WithTimeout chain, size the parent for the worst case across every child that inherits it — scoping to "the lane I'm implementing" instead of "every lane sharing this parent" is an easy, easy-to-miss bug.
