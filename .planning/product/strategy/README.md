## Strategic Overview

**Thesis:** OSS-first, get adoption, then monetize. ATCR has real potential in a genuine niche: multi-model code review with deterministic reconciliation and adversarial verification.

**The 4 phases:**

1. **Phase 1 (0-3 months): Build the magnet**
   - Disagreement Radar, Local Leaderboard, CI Integration, Persona Ecosystem seed
   - Goal: 100+ GitHub stars, 10+ contributors, leaderboard cited
   - Revenue: $0 (investment)

2. **Phase 2 (3-6 months): Services fund the OSS**
   - Model Selection Consulting ($5k-20k/engagement)
   - Enterprise Persona Development ($10k-50k/persona)
   - Strategic partnerships (Synthetic.new referral revenue)
   - Goal: 5+ consulting clients, $30k+ revenue
   - Revenue: $10k-100k

3. **Phase 3 (6-12 months): Product scales the business**
   - Review-as-a-Service API ($99-499/mo)
   - Team Edition + Compliance ($10k-50k/year per org)
   - Public Leaderboard (free, top-of-funnel)
   - Goal: 50+ paying customers, $20k+ MRR
   - Revenue: $50k-500k

4. **Phase 4 (12+ months): Premium features + enterprise**
   - Finding Remediation (auto-fix)
   - Review Intelligence Analytics
   - "Survived-a-Skeptic" Certification
   - Reconciler Library
   - Goal: 100+ paying customers, $50k+ MRR
   - Revenue: $100k-1M+

**The moat:** Multi-model + deterministic reconciler + adversarial verification. Most competitors are single-model or rule-based. Blue ocean opportunities: certification, consulting, reconciler library.

**Key insight:** Services are the bridge. Consulting generates revenue without breaking OSS trust. You're selling expertise, not the tool. This funds OSS development until the product plays (SaaS, enterprise) are ready.

## Decision Points

- **Month 3:** Go/No-Go on Services (100+ stars, 10+ contributors, 3+ blog posts)
- **Month 6:** Go/No-Go on SaaS (5+ consulting clients, $30k+ revenue, 200+ stars)
- **Month 12:** Go/No-Go on Enterprise (50+ paying customers, $20k+ MRR, 500+ stars)
